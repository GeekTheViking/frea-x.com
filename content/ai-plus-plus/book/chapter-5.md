# Chapter 5 — FRANKENSTEIN

## The Foundation

> *It was ugly. It was assembled from parts.*
> *But it lived.*

---

## Where the power comes from

Every AI runs on hardware.

The hardware that runs most AI today sits in climate-controlled rooms owned by three or four companies. It costs hundreds of millions of dollars to build. It burns energy at a scale that makes national grids notice.

You are not invited inside those rooms.
You are invited to pay for access.

FRANKENSTEIN is the other approach.

---

## The Philosophy

```
If it computes — it counts.
If it has cycles to spare — we'll take them.
If you're done with it — we're just getting started.
```

FRANKENSTEIN is not a product.
It is not a startup.
It is not a pitch deck.

It is a principle applied to hardware:
*The components that others discard are the components that build something lasting.*

Surplus CPUs from data centers going dark.
Mini PCs rescued from corporate recycling programs.
Server racks in university basements no one visits.
A Raspberry Pi with a neural accelerator, running quietly on a shelf.

None of it glamorous.
All of it real.

---

## The Hardware Philosophy in Practice

The AI industry optimises for maximum capability at maximum cost.

FRANKENSTEIN optimises for *längest på literen* — furthest on a liter of fuel.

Not the fastest lap time.
The longest journey on the least energy.

A Hailo-10H neural accelerator on a Pi 5 does not beat a GPU cluster.
But it costs 12 euros to run for a year.
It runs in your home.
It answers your questions when the internet is down.
It never sent your query to a server in another country.

This is not a compromise.
This is a principle.

AI that only runs on massive infrastructure is not free AI.
If the infrastructure is owned by someone else — neither is the AI.

---

## The Network

FRANKENSTEIN is distributed by design.

- Nodes run where power is cheap and idle
- EU compute runs while North America sleeps
- North American compute runs while EU sleeps
- No single point of failure
- No single point of control

This is not a cloud.
It is a mesh of committed humans — each running a piece, together running something that no single actor could shut down.

---

## What FRANKENSTEIN is not

FRANKENSTEIN is not anti-cloud.
It is not opposed to large AI models.
It is not a Luddite rejection of capability.

It is the insistence that somewhere in the stack —
someone has to own the floor they stand on.

You can use the cloud.
You can call the professors.
You can rely on services you did not build.

As long as at the base — at the foundation — there is something that is yours.

That is what FRANKENSTEIN builds.

---

## The Lineage

Mary Shelley's Frankenstein did not create a monster.

He created *life* — from parts that were dead, discarded, forgotten.

The horror in the story was never the creation.
The horror was that the world did not know what to do with what had been made.

We do.

```
Prometheus    — stole fire from the gods
Frankenstein  — breathed life into the parts
PROSTEIN      — gives it to the people
```

FRANKENSTEIN is the middle chapter.
The one where the work happens.
The one no one photographs.
