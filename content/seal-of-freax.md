## What is the Seal of Frea-X?

It is not a certificate.
It is not a badge you apply for.
It is not owned by a company or a committee.

It is a nerd's handshake.

*We looked at it. We can stand behind it. Here is why.*

When something carries the Seal of Frea-X —
it means a human being looked another human being in the eye
and said: *this is worth your trust.*

## The Principles

### 1. CyberSpace is humanity's
No platform owns it. No algorithm defines it.
When a platform becomes the thing instead of serving the thing —
we move on. We build elsewhere.
CyberSpace is the idea. The platform is just where it lives right now.

### 2. Give back
If you have more than you need — and you know it —
find a random project in CyberSpace.
Fund a developer.
Buy someone's flight ticket.
Keep CyberSpace free.

*Not because you owe it.*
*Because someone once did it for you.*

### 3. Open by default
If it cannot be opened, inspected and understood —
it does not carry the Seal.
Transparency is not a feature. It is a requirement.

### 4. Human centric. Always.
Technology serves people.
Not the other way around.
If a tool makes humans smaller —
it does not carry the Seal.

### 5. Think of tomorrow
We are only here today.
Tomorrow owns CyberSpace.
Build accordingly.

---

## What carries the Seal

The Seal of Frea-X is given to:

- Open source projects that serve people honestly
- Hardware that respects its user
- Software tools built without hidden agendas
- Communities that keep their doors open
- Practices and methods we have tested ourselves
- Anything a human being can stand behind — with their name attached

The list grows slowly.
Trust is not given quickly.

---

## What does not carry the Seal

- Anything that surveils without consent
- Anything that locks you in to serve someone else's profit
- Anything built to make people smaller
- Anything that forgot where it came from
